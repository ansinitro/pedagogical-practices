The Division Algorithm

For any integer \( a \) (dividend) and positive integer \( b \) (divisor), there exist unique integers \( q \) (quotient) and \( r \) (remainder) such that:

$$ a = bq + r $$ $$ 0 \le r < |b| $$

Our goal is to find \( q \) and \( r \) for given values of \( a \) and \( b \).

Task 5(a)

Find the quotient \( q \) and remainder \( r \) for:

$$ a = 5286 $$ $$ b = 19 $$

Solution 5(a)

1. Estimate Quoteint: Divide \( 5286 \) by \( 19 \):

$$ \frac{5286}{19} \approx 278.21 $$

So, we let \( q = 278 \).

2. Calculate Remainder:

$$ r = a - (b \cdot q) $$

$$ r = 5286 - (19 \cdot 278) = 5286 - 5282 = 4 $$

Final Equation:

$$ 5286 = 19(278) + 4 $$

Task 5(b)

Find \( q \) and \( r \) for:

$$ a = -5286 $$ $$ b = 19 $$

Hint: For negative \( a \), quotient \( q = \lfloor a/b \rfloor \). Round down (more negative).

Solution 5(b)

Method: When \( a \) is negative, normal division gives a negative remainder. We must decrease \( q \) by 1 to make \( r \) positive.

$$ 19 \cdot (-278) - 4 $$

Adjust by subtracting 1 from quotient and adding divisor to remainder:

$$ 19 \cdot (-278 - 1) + (19 - 4) $$

$$ q = -279, \quad r = 15 $$

$$ -5286 = 19(-279) + 15 $$

Task 5(c)

Find \( q \) and \( r \) for:

$$ a = 5286 $$ $$ b = -19 $$

Note: \( b \) is negative.

Solution 5(c)

Method: Adjust the sign of \( q \) to account for the negative divisor.

$$ 5286 = (-19) \cdot q + r $$

Since \( 5286 = 19(278) + 4 \), we can write:

$$ 5286 = (-19)(-278) + 4 $$

Result: \( q = -278, \ r = 4 \).

Check: \( 0 \le 4 < |-19| \) (Valid)

$$ 5286 = -19(-278) + 4 $$

Task 5(d)

Find \( q \) and \( r \) for:

$$ a = -5286 $$ $$ b = -19 $$

Note: Both \( a \) and \( b \) are negative.

Solution 5(d)

Method: Convert negative division into positive remainder.

Initially: \( -5286 = (-19)(278) - 4 \)

We need positive remainder. Add one more group of \( -19 \) (which is subtracting) to quotient? No, we need to add/sub wisely.

Actually: \( (-19) \cdot 278 = -5282 \). We need -4 to get -5286. Remainder -4 is invalid.

Increase q to 279: \( (-19)(279) = -5301 \). Then \( -5286 - (-5301) = 15 \).

$$ -5286 = -19(279) + 15 $$
