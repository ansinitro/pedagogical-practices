Task 17: Linear Diophantine Equations

Solving \( ax + by = c \) for integers \( x, y \).

The Condition

A Linear Diophantine Equation \( ax + by = c \) has integer solutions if and only if:

$$ \gcd(a, b) \mid c $$

If solutions exist, they are of the form:

\( x = x_0 + \frac{b}{d}t \), \( y = y_0 - \frac{a}{d}t \)

Task 17(a)

Solve for \( x, y \):

$$ 17369x + 5472y = 4 $$

Solution (a)

1. Euclidean Algorithm (Find GCD):

$$ 17369 = 5472(3) + 953 $$ $$ 5472 = 953(5) + 707 $$ $$ 953 = 707(1) + 246 $$ $$ 707 = 246(2) + 215 $$ $$ 246 = 215(1) + 31 $$ $$ 215 = 31(6) + 29 $$ $$ 31 = 29(1) + 2 $$ $$ 29 = 2(14) + 1 $$ $$ 2 = 1(2) + 0 $$

$$ \gcd(17369, 5472) = 1 $$

Since \( 1 \mid 4 \), solutions exist.

2. Back Substitution (Found 1):

Working backwards yields:

$$ 1 = 17369(-2647) + 5472(8402) $$

Multiply entire equation by 4 to match \( c=4 \):

$$ 4 = 17369(-10588) + 5472(33608) $$

So \( x_0 = -10588, \quad y_0 = 33608 \).

3. General Solution:

\( x = -10588 + 5472t \)

\( y = 33608 - 17369t \)

We want smaller base solutions. Let \( t' = t - 2 \). Then:

\( x = -10588 + 5472(2) + 5472t' = 356 + 5472t' \)

(Note: \( -10588 \equiv 356 \pmod{5472} \))

$$ x = 356 + 5472k $$
$$ y = -1130 - 17369t $$

Task 17(b)

Solve for \( x, y \):

$$ 154x + 260y = 4 $$

Solution (b)

1. GCD:

\( 260 = 154(1) + 106 \implies \dots \implies 10 = 8(1) + 2 \)

\( \gcd = 2 \). Since \( 2 \mid 4 \), sol exists.

2. Particular Sol:

Working backwards from \( 2 = 10 - 8(1) \dots \)

\( 2 = 16(260) - 27(154) \)

Multiply by 2 for \( c=4 \):

\( 4 = 32(260) - 54(154) \)

\( x_0 = -54, y_0 = 32 \)

3. General Sol:

$$ x = -54 + 130t $$
$$ y = 32 - 77t $$

Task 17(c)

Solve for \( x, y \):

$$ 154x + 260y = 3 $$

Solution (c)

1. GCD:

From Part (b), we know \( \gcd(154, 260) = 2 \).

2. Check Condition:

Does the GCD divide the constant term \( c = 3 \)?

$$ 2 \nmid 3 $$

No Integer Solutions.
