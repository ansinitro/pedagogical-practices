Section 3: Primes & Divisors

Primes, Composites & \( d(n) \)

Task 1(a)

Is \( 157 \) a prime number?

Solution 1(a)

Method: Square Root Test.

\( \sqrt{157} \approx 12.53 \)

Check primes \( p \) such that \( 1 < p < 12.53 \):

2, 3, 5, 7, 11

None of these divide 157.

Conclusion: 157 is Prime.

Task 1(b)

Is \( 9831 \) a prime number?

Solution 1(b)

Method: Sum of Digits (Divisibility by 3).

\( 9 + 8 + 3 + 1 = 21 \)

Since \( 21 \) is divisible by 3, \( 9831 \) is divisible by 3.

\( 9831 \equiv 21 \equiv 0 \pmod 3 \)

Conclusion: 9831 is Not Prime.

Task 1(c)

Is \( 9833 \) a prime number?

Solution 1(c)

Method: Square Root Test.

\( \sqrt{9833} \approx 99.16 \)

Check any prime \( n \) where \( 2 \le n < 99 \).

Upon testing, no prime less than 99 divides 9833.

Conclusion: 9833 is Prime.

Task 1(d)

Is \( 55551111 \) a prime number?

Solution 1(d)

Method: Sum of Digits.

\( 5+5+5+5+1+1+1+1 = 24 \)

\( 24 \) is divisible by 3.

\( 55551111 \equiv 24 \equiv 0 \pmod 3 \)

Conclusion: 55551111 is Not Prime.

Task 1(e)

Is \( 2^{216000} - 1 \) a prime number?

Solution 1(e)

Method: Difference of Squares.

$$ 2^{216000} - 1 = (2^{108000})^2 - 1^2 $$
$$ = (2^{108000} - 1)(2^{108000} + 1) $$

It can be factored into two integers greater than 1.

Conclusion: Not Prime.

Problem 17

Let \( d(n) \) be the number of positive divisors of \( n \).

(a) If \( d(n) = 2 \), what is \( n \)?

(b) If \( d(n) = 3 \), what is \( n \)?

Solution 17

(a) \( d(n) = 2 \)

Only prime numbers have exactly 2 divisors (1 and itself).

Conclusion: \( n \) is Prime.

(b) \( d(n) = 3 \)

Divisors must be \( 1, p, p^2 \).

If \( n \) had two distinct prime factors \( p, q \), divisors > 3.

Conclusion: \( n \) is the Square of a Prime (\( p^2 \)).
