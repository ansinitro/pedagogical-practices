Discrete Mathematics

Section 2: Integers

Task 12: GCD & Linear Combination

Euclidean Algorithm & Extended Euclidean Algorithm

The Task

Find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of \( a \) and \( b \), and express it in the form:

$$ \gcd(a, b) = ma + nb $$

Where \( m \) and \( n \) are suitable integers (Bézout coefficients).

Task 12(e)

Find GCD and \( m, n \) for:

$$ a = 1575 $$ $$ b = -231 $$

Solution (e)

1. Euclidean Algorithm:

\( 1575 = (-231)(-6) + 189 \)

\( -231 = 189(-2) + 147 \)

\( 189 = 147(1) + 42 \)

\( 147 = 42(3) + 21 \)

\( 42 = 21(2) + 0 \)

GCD is 21.

2. Linear Combination (Backwards):

\( 21 = 147 - 42(3) \)

\( 21 = 147 - (189 - 147)(3) = 147(4) - 189(3) \)

\( 21 = [-231 - 189(-2)](4) - 189(3) = -231(4) + 189(5) \)

\( 21 = -231(4) + [1575 - (-231)(-6)](5) \)

$$ 21 = 1575(5) + (-231)(34) $$

So \( m = 5, n = 34 \).

Task 12(g)

Find GCD and \( m, n \) for:

$$ a = -3719 $$ $$ b = 8416 $$

Solution (g)

1. Euclidean Algorithm:

\( -3719 = 8416(-1) + 4697 \)

\( 8416 = 4697(1) + 3719 \)

\( 4697 = 3719(1) + 978 \)

\( 3719 = 978(3) + 785 \)

\( 978 = 785(1) + 193 \)

\( 785 = 193(4) + 13 \)

\( 193 = 13(14) + 11 \)

\( 13 = 11(1) + 2 \)

\( 11 = 2(5) + 1 \)

\( 2 = 1(2) + 0 \)

The non-zero last remainder is 1. So GCD is 1.

2. Linear Combination (Back Substitution):

\( 1 = 11 - 5 \cdot 2 \)

\( = 11 - 5(13 - 11) = 6 \cdot 11 - 5 \cdot 13 \)

\( = 6(193 - 14 \cdot 13) - 5 \cdot 13 = 6 \cdot 193 - 89 \cdot 13 \)

\( = 6 \cdot 193 - 89(785 - 4 \cdot 193) = 362 \cdot 193 - 89 \cdot 785 \)

\( = 362(978 - 785) - 89 \cdot 785 = 362 \cdot 978 - 451 \cdot 785 \)

\( = 362 \cdot 978 - 451(3719 - 3 \cdot 978) = 1715 \cdot 978 - 451 \cdot 3719 \)

\( = 1715(4697 - 3719) - 451 \cdot 3719 = 1715 \cdot 4697 - 2166 \cdot 3719 \)

\( = 1715(8416 - 3719) - 2166 \cdot 3719 = 1715 \cdot 8416 - 3881 \cdot 3719 \)

\( = 3881(-3719) + 1715(8416) \)

\( 1 = 3881(-3719) + 1715(8416) \)

So \( m = 3881, n = 1715 \).
