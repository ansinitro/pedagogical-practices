Basic Modulo, Linear Congruences & Fermat's Little Theorem

Task 3

Find \( a \pmod n \) for:

  • (a) \( a = 1286, n = 39 \)
  • (b) \( a = 43197, n = 333 \)
  • (c) \( a = -545608, n = 51 \)
  • (d) \( a = -125617, n = 315 \)
  • (e) \( a = 111,111,111,111, n = 1111 \)

Solution Task 3

(a) \( 1286 = 39(32) + 38 \implies 1286 \equiv 38 \pmod{39} \)

(b) \( 43197 = 333(129) + 240 \implies 43197 \equiv 240 \pmod{333} \)

(c) \( -545608 \pmod{51} \)

Recall: \( a = nq + r \). For negatives, round quotient down.

\( -545608 = 51(-10699) + 41 \)

\( \implies -545608 \equiv 41 \pmod{51} \)

(d) \( -125617 \pmod{315} \)

\( -125617 = 315(-399) + 68 \)

\( \implies -125617 \equiv 68 \pmod{315} \)

(e) \( 111...111 = 1111(100010000) + 111 \implies 111...111 \equiv 111 \pmod{1111} \)

Task 7(a) Operations

Given:

\( a=4003, b=-127, n=85 \)

Find:

  • \( (a+b) \pmod{n} \)
  • \( ab \pmod{n} \)
  • \( (a+b)^2 \pmod{n} \)

Solution Task 7(a)

1. \( a+b \):

\( 4003 - 127 = 3876 \)

\( 3876 = 85(45) + 51 \implies 3876 \equiv 51 \pmod{85} \)

2. \( ab \):

\( 4003(-127) = -508381 \)

\( -508381 = 85(-5981) + 4 \implies ab \equiv 4 \pmod{85} \)

3. \( (a+b)^2 \):

\( 51^2 = 2601 \)

\( 2601 = 85(30) + 51 \implies (a+b)^2 \equiv 51 \pmod{85} \)

Task 9: Linear Congruences

Solve for \( x \):

  • (a) \( 3x \equiv 4 \pmod 6 \)
  • (b) \( 4x \equiv 2 \pmod 6 \)
  • (c) \( 4x \equiv 3 \pmod 7 \)
  • (d) \( 4x \equiv 3 \pmod 6 \)

Solution Task 9

(a) \( 3x \equiv 4 \pmod 6 \)

x 0 1 2 3 4 5
3x 0 3 6≡0 9≡3 12≡0 15≡3

No x gives 4. No Solution.

(b) \( 4x \equiv 2 \pmod 6 \)

x 0 1 2 3 4 5
4x 0 4 8≡2 12≡0 16≡4 20≡2

Solutions: x = 2, x = 5.

(c) \( 4x \equiv 3 \pmod 7 \):

Inverse of 4 mod 7 is 2. \( x \equiv 3(2) \equiv 6 \).

x = 6.

(d) \( 4x \equiv 3 \pmod 6 \):

4x is even, 3 is odd. Impossible.

No Solution.

Task 22(b): Fermat's Little Theorem

Evaluate:

$$ 53^{20592} \pmod{20593} $$

Theorem: If \( p \) is prime, \( a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod p \).

Solution Task 22(b)

By Fermat's Little Theorem:

If \( \gcd(a, p) = 1 \) and \( p \) is prime, then:

$$ a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \pmod p $$

Since \( 20593 \) is prime and \( \gcd(53, 20593)=1 \):

$$ 53^{20592} \equiv 1 \pmod{20593} $$

Extensions:

  • \( 53^{20593} = 53 \cdot 53^{20592} \equiv 53 \cdot 1 \equiv 53 \pmod{20593} \)
  • \( 53^{20594} = 53^2 \cdot 53^{20592} \equiv 2809 \cdot 1 \equiv 2809 \pmod{20593} \)
