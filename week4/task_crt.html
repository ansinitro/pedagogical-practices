Section 5: Chinese Remainder Theorem

Solving Systems of Linear Congruences

Problem 18(a)

Solve the System:

$$ \begin{cases} x \equiv 3 \pmod 5 \\ x \equiv 4 \pmod 7 \end{cases} $$

Solution 18(a)

1. Calculate N:

\( N = 5 \times 7 = 35 \).

2. Setup Formula:

\( x = \frac{35}{5}(3)b_1 + \frac{35}{7}(4)b_2 \pmod{35} \)

\( x = 21b_1 + 20b_2 \pmod{35} \)

3. Find Inverses:

  • \( 7b_1 \equiv 1 \pmod 5 \implies 2b_1 \equiv 1 \pmod 5 \implies b_1 \equiv 3 \pmod 5 \)
  • \( 5b_2 \equiv 1 \pmod 7 \implies b_2 \equiv 3 \pmod 7 \)

4. Final Calculation:

\( x = 21(3) + 20(3) \pmod{35} = 63 + 60 \pmod{35} = 123 \pmod{35} \)

\( 123 \pmod{35} \equiv 18 \pmod{35} \)

Result: \( x \equiv 18 \pmod{35} \)

Problem 18(c)

Solve the System:

$$ \begin{cases} x \equiv 3 \pmod 5 \\ x \equiv 7 \pmod 8 \end{cases} $$

Solution 18(c)

1. Calculate N:

\( N = 5 \times 8 = 40 \).

2. Setup Formula:

\( x = \frac{40}{5}(3)b_1 + \frac{40}{8}(7)b_2 \pmod{40} \)

\( x = 24b_1 + 35b_2 \pmod{40} \)

3. Find Inverses:

  • \( 8b_1 \equiv 1 \pmod 5 \implies 3b_1 \equiv 1 \pmod 5 \implies b_1 \equiv 2 \pmod 5 \)
  • \( 5b_2 \equiv 1 \pmod 8 \implies b_2 \equiv 5 \pmod 8 \)

4. Final Calculation:

\( x = 24(2) + 35(5) \pmod{40} = 48 + 175 \pmod{40} = 223 \pmod{40} \)

\( 223 \pmod{40} \equiv 23 \pmod{40} \)

Result: \( x \equiv 23 \pmod{40} \)
